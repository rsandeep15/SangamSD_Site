Aasha Sachdev



Aasha Sachdev, Founding Member SangamSD | What motivated you to start Sangam? ‘In 1986, my friends and I noticed that there were organizations for many other ethnicities at UCSD, but none for us (South Asian ethnicity). We were a small minority in our high schools, as well as at UCSD (different from today!). We also had noticed that many people around us had little exposure to our culture, though no fault of their own. We spent the first year gathering members, setting up small cultural shows with dance (and musical) demonstrations, screening Hindi Art films, and integrating our organization into UCSD events. Sangam sold samosas at the Sun God Festival!’ What are your favorite Sangam memories from your college years? ‘We liked the opportunities to put on our festive/cultural bright clothes and shine with pride. Our parents were pleasantly surprised that we were embracing all of this. We also had great events and parties! Coming up with a variety of events encompassing both culture and FUN was a challenge. We also pulled in other ethnic groups and tried not to isolate our organization to only our ethnic group.’ Why did you choose Sangam (Sanskrit word meaning convergence/intersection) as the name of this organization? ‘We had heard various definitions of togetherness, of coming together, and finally it (Sangam) sounded right to us. We were from various parts of India, Pakistan, (and other South Asian nations). We also had friends that were not from these ethnicities, but were strong supporters of our organization. At the time, we were a small group on campus, so we enjoyed the curiosity our name generated.’ When you look at the continued success that Sangam has actively exhibited for the past 30 years, do you feel as if your vision for Sangam has been fulfilled? ‘First, I would say ‘our’ as I was one of many that pulled it together to make it a reality. My friends and I wanted an organization to embrace our culture and educate others about our culture in a fun way, and we fulfilled our wish. As for where it is today, Sangam has far exceeded what we could have dreamed. It is wonderful to see each successive class do more with an idea born from a group of friends hanging out on the grass at UCSD in 1986!’

Rishabh Malhotra



Rishabh Malhotra, 1st Year, Computer Science | What would you say is one of your biggest weakness and strength? Weakness ‘I would say one of my biggest weakness is that I am very bad at time management. I am trying to improve that aspect of my life because a lot of my current problems are occurring because of this weakness of mine. I feel that if I were to improve on that, I will have less stress on my mind and won’t have this feeling of a heavy weight on my shoulders. It’s just a problem that I have to overcome.’ Strength ‘I would say that I am a determined person. If there is something on my mind, I will strive to great levels to get what I want. For instances, within these past couple of weeks I wanted to go to the gym more regularly so I can become more fit and healthy. In order to achieve this goal, I would start off going to the gym four days a week. As this lifestyle started to be a routine of mine, I increased my number of days from four to six days a week. I have achieved what I set my mind to. This also plays into my academic life. Even though I have bad time management, I am determined to finish what I was assigned and try my level best to stay on track.’

Sarin Parikh



Sarin Parikh, 4th Year, Nanoengineering | If you had a year to live, what would you achieve over the next 12 months? ‘Lets just assume I am old and loaded. So anything can be possible here. I would love to travel the world. Though I am not the one to do something over the top or crazy but I do have an interest in meeting different kinds of people and exploring all the different cultures that is out there in our world. First choice? ‘I don’t have a first choice place. But I would love to go to Europe because it has my top ten places I would love to explore: Britain, France, etc. I would also like to go to Sweden, Turkey, and Japan. I also think it would also be interesting to go Antarctica to see penguins.’

Divya Raman



Divya Raman, 1st Year, Human Biology | If you could wish for anything, what would it be? ‘If I could wish for anything in my life, it would be to be content because I feel that people are so focused about the future or are anxious about what is going to happen in their life that they forget to focus on being content in the present state. My wish is to have everyone let go on what is preoccupying their mind and just focus on what life is giving them today. I understand that this isn’t easy because I even struggle with this. But I truly believe that if we all were to focus on the present and be content with what life has brought us, we will all be happier individuals.’

Parthu Reddy Kalva



Parthu Reddy Kalva, 5th Year, Physiology/Neuroscience and Public Health | Everyone has a weakness or a fear. What would you say is your fear? ‘The only fear I have is that I am going to run out of energy. I think a lot of people in this country say that time is money but I think energy is money. I think you can manage your time, you can manage your money, but managing your energy is very difficult. I am scared that the obligations that life will bring in the future will eventually lead to me running out of my source of energy. This scares me because I feel that once I have used up my energy I am not going to have any left to do the stuff that I personally feel like I am here, on this Earth, to do. The type of stuff that keeps me waking up each morning, the type of stuff that makes me eager and gets my heart beating fast are the things that I want to spend my energy on. I fear that after I go through graduate school, jobs, making money and making a family, I am going to not have any energy left and will look back and wonder where my life went. For me, I hope that I have the energy and the optimism after graduate school to finish what I started and I really hope that this isn’t proven to be my biggest obstacle.’

Milan Das



Milan Das, Sophomore, Engineering | What is your motivation? What keeps you going with everything you do? ‘My family is my motivation because they always push me to do things that are outside of my box, which ultimately helps me grow as a person. I have a lot of older cousins who have always guided me to do everything I possibly can and strive for success each time. Along with my cousins, my parents provide me with guidance and encouragement to push myself to be the best I can ever be. I feel like that is my motivation to not disappoint them. Though I have a lot of older relatives to guide me to the right path, I have my two younger brothers and younger cousin that look up to me, which serves as another motivation of mine. I want them to keep that vision of me so I could always be their motivation. I try my best to make all my family members proud.’

Parth Sheth



Parth Sheth, Junior, Biochemistry and Cell Biology | Everyone has a story. What would your story be? ‘My UCSD career revolved around joining UCSD Raas Ruckus. Though I never had danced before my freshman year, it was a really good experience for me. It had allowed me to push myself to be the best dancer I could ever be. It started becoming a passion of mine, which is why I became captain of this team the following year. Being captain has taught me a lot about leadership and the ways in which I can lead a group of people to success. It was the same year that we won first place at GWA. This victory was really special to me because we hadn’t won in a really long time and it comes to show that all our hard work was rewarded. That win meant a lot to me. Not only has this become a passion of mine but it has also complemented my premed work. I used dance practices and competitions as a stress reliever from all my premed pressures. I really miss being on the team this year and hopefully I will be on the team next year.’
